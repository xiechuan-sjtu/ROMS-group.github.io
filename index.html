Ross Sea-Amundsen Sea Model (RAS model)

 

The Ross Sea-Amundsen Sea high-resolution ocean-sea ice-ice shelf model (RAS model) was developed based on the Regional Ocean Modeling System (ROMS v3.6). The RAS model domain covers the entire Ross Sea and Amundsen Sea including the portion underneath the floating ice shelves. The domain extends from roughly 85.6°S to 64.2°S, 143.0°E to 89.9°W. The model resolution is about 3 km in both horizontal dimensions and includes 32 vertical levels, which are concentrated toward the top and bottom surfaces.

Overview

  • Model Description
    The model bathymetry and ice shelf draft have been interpolated from MEaSUREs BedMachine Antarctica, Version 2.(MEaSUREs)

  • Atmosphere forcing
    Atmospheric forcing are taken from the ERA5 reanalysis data and consists of wind vectors, sea level pressure, humidity, air temperature, cloud cover, precipitation rate and short-wave radiation.(ERA5)

  • Boundary conditions
    The temperature, salinity, sea surface height and depth averaged velocity for the open boundaries were specified as daily means derived from the Met Office Global Seasonal forecasting system version 5. (Glosea5)
    Sea ice concentration. (AMSRE/AMSR2)

  • Initial conditions
    Initial conditions of temperature and salinity are taken from the 10-km horizontal-resolution circum-Antarctic ocean–sea ice–ice shelf model. (Dinniman et al., 2015)
